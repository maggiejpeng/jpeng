About Maggie

I was the kid who annoyed the shit out of everyone because I would never stop asking why. My teacher from pre-k to college can attest to that, and in many ways I'm still that kid. Now I also love doing what Malcom Gladwell calls, "connecting the dots," solving problems by asking good questions that clarify reality and identify the common thread in situations, cultures, personalities and problems.

Born in Szechuan (yes the word synonymous with spicy on Chinese menu), bred in Shanghai, I spent my formative years of high school in Connecticut daydreaming a lot, debating ideas to and fro classes and that freedom made me a malleable, adaptable and unapologetic questioner. I chose my major by process of elimination and there was only one option left, advertising. As a cosmopolitan, the diversity of my experience has always pushed me to always try harder to understand the other side. I learned the necessity of fostering a nuanced curiosity for people and culture, and the same goes when I conduct research as a strategist or go about solving business/human problems when they're not readily relatable.

I'm currently a first-time cat mom, taking care of two kitties for my friend.
